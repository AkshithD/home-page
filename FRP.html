In-depth

  • Kemp TW, Dunlop JS, McLure RJ, Schreiber C, Carnall AC, Cullen F. Maximizing the power of deep extragalactic imaging surveys with the James Webb Space Telescope. Monthly notices of the Royal Astronomical Society. 2019;486(3):3087-3104. doi:10.1093/mnras/stz1038

    Kemp et al. (2019) discuss the potential of using the James Webb Space Telescope to maximize deep extragalactic imaging surveys.

  • Beichman C, Benneke B, Knutson H, et al. Observations of Transiting Exoplanets with the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST). Publications of the Astronomical Society of the Pacific. 2014;126(946):1134-1173. doi:10.1086/679566

    Beichman et al. (2014) present observations of transiting exoplanets using the James Webb Space Telescope, exploring the characteristics of their atmospheres.

  • James Webb Space Telescope: Technical Challenges Have Caused Schedule Strain and May Increase Costs: Report to Congressional Committees. United States Government Accountability Office; 2020.

    The report by the United States Government Accountability Office (2020) highlights technical challenges and cost increases related to the James Webb Space Telescope.

General Interest

  • James Webb Space Telescope Science Guide. Edition 1.2. Space Telescope Science Institute; 2012.

    The James Webb Space Telescope Science Guide (2012) provides an overview of the scientific objectives and capabilities of the telescope.

  • Beichman C, Benneke B, Knutson H, et al. Observations of Transiting Exoplanets with the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST). Publications of the Astronomical Society of the Pacific. 2014;126(946):1134-1173. doi:10.1086/679566

    Another article by Beichman et al. (2014) focuses on observations of transiting exoplanets, specifically using the James Webb Space Telescope to improve the accuracy of their atmospheric measurements.

  • Keszthelyi L, Grundy W, Stansberry J, et al. Observing Outer Planet Satellites (Except Titan) with the James Webb Space Telescope: Science Justification and Observational Requirements. Publications of the Astronomical Society of the Pacific. 2016;128(959):1-8. doi:10.1088/1538-3873/128/959/018006

    Keszthelyi et al. (2016) discuss the scientific justification and observational requirements for using the James Webb Space Telescope to observe outer planet satellites (except Titan).

Key Interest

  • “Who Is James Webb - Webb/NASA.” NASA, NASA, https://webb.nasa.gov/content/about/faqs/whoIsJamesWebb.html.

    NASA's "Who Is James Webb" webpage provides information about the man who the James Webb Space Telescope is named after and his contributions to the space program.

  • “The Nobel Prize in Physics 2006.” NobelPrize.org, https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2006/mather/biographical/.

    The Nobel Prize in Physics (2006) was awarded to John Mather and George Smoot for their work on the Cosmic Background Explorer (COBE), a project that laid the foundation for the development of the James Webb Space Telescope.

  • “Amber Straughn Biography Webb Telescope/NASA.” NASA, NASA, ttps://webb.nasa.gov/content/meetTheTeam/people/straughn.html.h

    Amber Straughn is an astrophysicist and member of the James Webb Space Telescope team, and her biography on NASA's website provides information about her background and involvement in the project.
